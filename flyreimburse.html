How To Get Compensation?

  1. Collect all relevant documents, such as your flight itinerary, boarding pass, and any communications with the airline regarding the disruption. This is important as it will serve as a proof of the disruption that occurred.
  2. Check if you are eligible for compensation. Different airlines and circumstances have different rules for compensation, so it’s important to understand what you may be entitled to before making a claim.
  3. Contact the airline as soon as possible and inform them of the disruption and your desire to claim for compensation. Please note that there may be time limits for making a claim and the sooner you notify the airline, the better.
  4. Provide the documentation and information requested by the airline to support your claim. This could include things like flight tickets, boarding passes, and any other relevant information that supports your claim.
  5. If the airline refuses to compensate, you may contact relevant regulatory bodies or find the best flight compensation company for assistance in pursuing your claim.

We Solve More Flight Problems

01

Delayed Flight Compensation

Time is money – if your flight was late to arrive at the final destination by more than 3 hours due to the airline’s fault, you are eligible for compensation.

02

Cancelled Flight Compensation

Notification is a must – if you weren’t notified of a flight cancellation at least 14 days in advance, you are eligible for compensation. Moreover, in addition to compensation, you may also be entitled to a refund of your ticket for the cancelled flight.

03

Overbooked Flight Compensation

A seat must be guaranteed – if you were denied boarding due to overbooking, you are also eligible for compensation. The compensation amount varies depending on the distance of the flight and the length of delay for re-routing.

04

Air Passenger Rights

Know your rights – don’t let the airlines trick you. Take your time to read European Regulation (EC) No. 261/2004 and learn about what you’re entitled to, including compensation, free services at the airport and ticket refunds.

Know Your Rights

What Is The Difference Between A Compensation And A Ticket Refund?

A compensation is a payment made to a passenger for inconvenience or damages caused by a flight disruption, while a ticket refund is the return of the cost of the ticket for a flight that was not taken or was cancelled. Flight claim companies might help you get both airline compensation and a refund.

After How Long Can Passenger Claims Still Be Enforced?

Passenger claims for compensation or refund can typically be enforced within a certain time frame, such as three years for EU flights, but this may vary depending on the jurisdiction and specific situation. So it’s recommended to file your claim for compensation at the earliest possibility.

How Do I Get Justice In Case Of A Flight Problem?

If the airline does not offer a satisfactory solution, consider contacting relevant authorities such as the Department of Transportation or the European Consumer Center for assistance or hire a lawyer for flight compensation. Alternatively, you might delegate all the hard work to one of the flight compensation companies.
